## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### 1. Total Claims

**Shows**: All claims in current period

- **Organization View** (Admin): All organization claims
- **Department View** (Manager): Department claims only
- **Personal View** (Employee): Claims you created

**Interpretation:**
- Increasing trend: Growing issue volume (hiring needed?)
- Decreasing trend: Process improvements working
- Seasonal patterns: Plan resource allocation
- Compare to previous period: Track growth/decline

**Actions:**
- Click to see claim list with filters
- Break down by department or category
- Analyze by priority level

### 2. Average Resolution Time

**Shows**: How long claims typically take from OPEN to CLOSED (in hours)

**Example metrics:**
- 12 hours: Excellent - very responsive
- 24-48 hours: Good - reasonable turnaround
- 72+ hours: Investigate - may need process review

**What affects resolution time:**
- Claim complexity
- Team availability
- SLA policies
- Escalation frequency

**Actions:**
- Click to drill down by category
- See which categories take longest
- Identify bottlenecks
- Compare teams

### 3. SLA Compliance Rate

- 95% compliance: Excellent

**Trends to watch:**
- Declining compliance: Need more resources
- Specific category declining: Process issue
- Specific team declining: Training needed

**Actions:**
- Click to see which claims missed SLA
- Filter by category or department
- Identify common issues

### 5. Category Breakdown

**Shows**: Claims by type (Quality, Safety, Logistics, Maintenance)

**Example distribution:**
- Quality: 40%
- Maintenance: 25%
- Logistics: 20%
- Safety: 10%
- Other: 5%

- Reflects operational reality of your facility
- Quality-heavy: Focus on manufacturing processes
- Maintenance-heavy: Equipment aging?

- Sudden increase in category: Root cause?
- Seasonal patterns: Weather, staffing, etc.
- New equipment = new category spike

- Click category to drill down
- See all claims in category
- Analyze trends over time

### 6. Department Performance

**Shows**: How each department is performing

**Metrics per department:**
- Total claims in department
- Average resolution time
- SLA compliance %
- Workload (claims per person)
- Trending

**Interpretation:**
- Consistent high SLA: Well-managed team
- Declining SLA: Needs support
- High workload + low SLA: Hiring needed
- Low activity: Team tracking properly?

**Actions:**
- Click department to see details
- Compare departments against each other
- Identify star performers
- Support struggling teams

### Resolution Time Trend

**Shows**: Is resolution getting faster or slower?

**Tracking:**
- Improving: Process getting better
- Declining: Maybe new staff less experienced?
- Stable: Consistent performance

**Cause investigation:**
- Staffing changes: New people slower initially
- Process changes: May help or hurt
- Equipment changes: Might affect repair time
- Seasonal: Holidays, weather impact

### SLA Compliance Trend

**Shows**: Meeting deadlines consistently?

**Healthy trend:**
- 90%+ consistently
- Small variations (85-95%)
- Upward movement: Good

**Concerning trend:**
- Below 80%
- Declining month over month
- Specific category dropping
- Specific team struggling

**Actions when SLA drops:**
1. What changed? (Staffing, process, volume)
2. Which claims are missing? (Category, department)
3. Can we fix quickly? (Add resources, simplify process)
4. Is it temporary or pattern? (Plan response accordingly)

### Category Trends

**Shows**: Are certain claim types increasing or decreasing?

**Questions to ask:**
- Quality increasing: Is manufacturing slipping?
- Safety increasing: Are hazards being discovered?
- Maintenance increasing: Equipment aging?
- Logistics increasing: Supply chain issues?

**Taking action:**
- Investigate root causes
- Make process changes
- Address systemic issues
- Celebrate improvements

## Advanced Analytics

### Root Cause Analysis

**Shows**: What causes most claims?

**Example breakdown:**
- Training gaps: 35% of safety claims
- Equipment age: 40% of maintenance claims
- Supplier quality: 25% of logistics claims
- Process errors: 30% of quality claims

**Strategic use:**
- Address top causes first (highest ROI)
- Target training on gap areas
- Plan equipment replacement
- Improve supplier relationships

### Repeat Issue Identification

**Shows**: Claims that repeat frequently

**Example:**
- "Conveyor belt tension" - 5 times in 6 months
- "Sensor calibration" - 3 times in 3 months
- "Supplier delivery delay" - 7 times in year

**Actions:**
- Implement permanent fix (not temporary)
- Update maintenance schedule
- Change supplier
- Update procedures

### Department Efficiency

**Shows**: How efficiently each department works

**Metrics:**
- Claims per person
- Average resolution time
- SLA compliance
- First-time fix rate (solves without escalation)

**Benchmarking:**
- Compare departments to each other
- Compare to industry standards
- Track improvements over time

## Troubleshooting Dashboard

### Metrics Seem Wrong

**Check these things:**
- Date range correct? (Make sure you're looking at right period)
- Filters applied? (Might be filtering out data)
- Timezone correct? (Timestamps in right zone)
- Claims marked properly? (Status and category correct)

### Missing Data

**Reasons:**
- Claims haven't been created yet (new organization)
- Time range too narrow (no data in that period)
- User permission limited (can't see some claims)
- Filters too restrictive

### Performance Slow

**Try:**
- Reduce time range (daily instead of yearly)
- Filter to specific department
- Close other browser tabs
- Refresh page
- Contact support if continues
